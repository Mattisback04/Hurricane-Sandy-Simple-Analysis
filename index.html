Hurricane Sandy Weather Event Analysis

A Synoptic Overview using Matplotlib and Cartopy

Group 3: Noelle Davis, Matthew Lentz, Gabe Slade

The Event (Oct 22 - Nov 1, 2012)

  • October 22, 2012: Sandy formed in the central Caribbean Sea.
  • Progression: It intensified into a hurricane while moving north across Jamaica, eastern Cuba, and the Bahamas.
  • October 28-29, 2012: After moving northeast parallel to the U.S. East Coast, the storm took an unusual westward turn toward the Mid-Atlantic.
  • Landfall: Made landfall near Atlantic City, NJ, on the evening of October 29, 2012, transitioning into a midlatitude cyclone soon thereafter.

Mean Sea Level Pressure & Winds

  • Isotach shading and wind barbs.
  • Max winds are generally less than 73 MPH due to poor model resolution of the GFS.
  • GFS forecasted sub-950mb MSLP at landfall.

Geopotential Heights & Moisture

  • Fujiwhara-style effect with an upper-level trough.
  • Contributions to extratropical features.
  • The 540 height line is where snowfall can occur!

Radar Reflectivity

  • Flooding rainfall potential.
  • Lack of visible "eye".
  • Nice counterclockwise spin!
